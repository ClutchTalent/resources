Use This Worksheet

+ Also see our 5-minute task list

Learn

  • Follow blogs by key contributors.
  • Find new trends: Google "alternatives to" for every language, framework and tool you use.
  • Sign up for a newsletter that is relevant to your work. Examples: --- JavaScript Weekly

Innovate at Work

  • Present and idea for how to make a process more efficient or cleaner.
  • Create a style guide.
  • Build a dashboard for a business team to allow them to collaborate better with engineering.

Write

  • Create your own blog.
  • Offer to write for a blog with an established following.
  • Answer questions on Stack Overflow or Quora and then reblog onto your own blog.

Speak, Teach, or Mentor

  • Give a talk or demo at work.
  • Offer to speak for your favorite local meetup or apply to present at a conference.
  • Mentor at a workshop, or find someone entering the field to mentor 1:1. Blog about your mentorship journey!

Improve Your Interview Skills

  • Practice, practice, practice: algorithms, data structures, language-specific, problem solving. Work through the book Cracking the coding interview
  • Practice verbal questions with a friend.
  • Try out engineering interview questions from popular companies on GeeksforGeeks.
  • Offer to be on your employer's interview team.

Make Open Source Contributions

  • Find a group to collaborate with, or join an existing project.
  • Contribute to one of Google's Open Source Projects.
  • Apply for a Github Open Source Mentorship.
  • Find a first-time contributors project on Your First PR.

Learn a New Language or Framework

  • Pick up a book.
  • Go through a tutorial.
  • Take a class.
  • Work on a personal project in a new language.

Leadership Skill Development

  • Build hard skills: agile process, how to delegate work across teams, how to conduct performance evaluations, architecture, etc.
  • Build soft skills, including developing management philosophies.

Expand Your Network

  • Find people at meetups, conferences and at work to talk with about their work and share ideas with.
  • Follow-up over LinkedIn and follow people you meet on Twitter.
  • Connect online, especially with small, local online communities.

Build Your Online Profile

  • Check what comes up when you google your name, and make that a priority to improve.
  • Improve your LinkedIn profile to include projects, top skills, links to your online contributions and social profiles and a summary.
  • Even a few social media posts across Twitter, etc can improve your online image.
